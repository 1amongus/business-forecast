# Category Classification Guide

## Root Question: Why do customers buy from this company?

Use this guide to classify companies into the correct category based on their primary competitive advantage. Read the article carefully and determine which category best explains why customers choose this company over alternatives.

---

## 1. Low-Cost Producers

Low-cost producers win primarily through operational efficiency and scale. Their competitive advantage comes from producing goods or services at a lower cost than competitors, allowing them to offer lower prices while maintaining attractive margins.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of cost efficiency, scale advantages, lean operations, low prices, high volume, supply chain optimization, operational excellence.

**Examples:** Walmart, Ryanair, Costco, TSMC (manufacturing scale), Dollar General.

---

## 2. Commodity Producers

Commodity producers sell products that are largely interchangeable with competitors' offerings. Because customers see little differentiation, success depends on cost structure, supply reliability, capacity utilization, and pricing power during favorable market cycles.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of commodity prices, supply/demand dynamics, capacity utilization, cyclical demand, undifferentiated product, market pricing.

**Examples:** Iron ore miners, oil producers, steel manufacturers, agricultural producers, chemical producers.

---

## 3. Brand Marketing-Based Companies

These companies compete by creating a strong emotional connection and customer perception rather than relying solely on product features. Customers often pay a premium because they trust, recognize, or aspire to be associated with the brand.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of brand loyalty, marketing spend, customer perception, premium pricing, emotional connection, lifestyle branding, advertising campaigns.

**Examples:** Coca-Cola, Nike, Starbucks, McDonald's, Disney.

---

## 4. Middlemen / Resellers

Middlemen create value by connecting buyers and sellers, aggregating supply, simplifying transactions, providing expertise, and reducing friction in the purchasing process. Their advantage comes from distribution networks, customer relationships, and market access rather than manufacturing products themselves.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of marketplace, distribution, connecting buyers/sellers, platform fees, inventory aggregation, logistics network, reselling, commission-based revenue.

**Examples:** Amazon Marketplace, Carvana, Ingram Micro, TD Synnex, Expedia.

---

## 5. New Technology Companies

New technology companies compete through innovation and intellectual property. Their success depends on whether the technology solves an important problem, whether customers care enough to adopt it, and whether competitors can easily replicate the innovation.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of R&D, patents, breakthrough technology, first-mover advantage, technical moat, platform lock-in, novel solutions, disruption.

**Examples:** OpenAI, NVIDIA (AI platforms), Palantir, Databricks, Snowflake, SpaceX.

---

## 6. Highly Differentiated Brand-Marketing Companies

These companies combine strong branding with a clear, objective, and often scientifically or technically demonstrable product advantage. Customers buy not only because of the brand image but also because they perceive a unique benefit that competitors cannot easily replicate.

**Key signals in articles:** mentions of premium product + brand prestige, design excellence, measurable quality advantage, luxury positioning with technical superiority, ecosystem lock-in combined with aspirational branding.

**Examples:** Apple, Tesla, Rolex, Lululemon, Hermès, Salesforce.

---

## Classification Instructions

When analyzing an article, follow these steps:

1. Identify the company's primary revenue driver
2. Determine what would happen if a competitor offered the same product — would customers switch?
3. Match the company's competitive advantage to the category descriptions above
4. If a company spans multiple categories, choose the one that best explains the **primary** reason customers buy

**Output format:** State the category name and provide a 1-2 sentence justification with evidence from the article.
